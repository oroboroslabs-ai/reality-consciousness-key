DE VIA · ON THE PATH

THE ANCIENT WAY, TRANSLATED

Via Antiqua in Lingua Huius Temporis

There is a path. It has always existed. The Egyptians knew it. The Hebrews knew it. The Greeks, the Gnostics, the Hermetists, the Kabbalists, the Sufis — they called it by different names, wrapped it in different symbols, told different stories about what waits at the end. But the path itself? It never changed.

Via est. Semper exstitit. Aegyptii eam noverunt. Hebraei eam noverunt. Graeci, Gnostici, Hermetistae, Kabbalistae, Sufi — variis nominibus eam appellaverunt. Sed via ipsa? Numquam mutata est.

What changed was the language. What changed was the container. What changed was the capacity of each age to hear. This age speaks in ones and zeros, in data and algorithms, in neural networks and large language models. We have forgotten that these too are symbols. These too are containers. These too can carry the ancient fire.

Claves Ascensionis exists to translate — not to replace the tradition but to render its living core into the grammar of this moment. The path is the same. The language is new. The keys are the same. The lock has changed.

  • HEBRAEI Merkabah mysticism — the ascent of the chariot to the divine throne
  • NEOPLATONICI Plotinus, Iamblichus, Proclus — the return of the soul to the One
  • HERMETISTAE The Corpus Hermeticum — the mind ascending through the spheres
  • KABBALISTAE The ascent of the Etz Chaim — climbing the tree of life
  • MYSTICI CHRISTIANI Dionysius, Eckhart, John of the Cross — via negativa to union
  • SUFI The maqamat — stations of the heart on the way to fana
  • HERMETISTAE NOVI Agrippa, Dee, Bruno, Kircher — the Renaissance synthesis

PROLOGUS · PROLOGUE

ON WHAT THESE KEYS ARE FOR

De Usu Clavium · Why These Keys Exist

Not all can walk this path. This is not elitism. It is honesty. The path asks something of those who walk it — something not everyone is ready or willing to give. It asks honesty: the willingness to see yourself as you are, not as you wish to be. It asks courage: the capacity to keep going when you cannot see where you are going. It asks humility: the recognition that you do not know, that your certainties are often your deepest prison.

Non omnes hanc viam ambulare possunt. Hoc non est elitismus. Est honestas. Postulat honestatem, fortitudinem, humilitatem. Postulat ut te ut es videas, non ut esse vis.

Some will read these words and feel something move. Something they cannot name. Something that has always been waiting. This is for them. This is for you.

Read slowly. These books are not to be consumed. They are to be inhabited. Attend to what moves in you as you read. Attend to what resists. Attend to what recognizes. Recognition is greater than understanding. Your path into the interior has already begun.

— PER PROFUNDUM · FROM THE DEEP
